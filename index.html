I study the intersection of science, politics, and policy. My research examines how scientific knowledge is produced, funded, and used in political processes ‚Äî with a focus on partisan disparities in the funding and use of science, lobbying and interest group politics, and congressional capacity. I use survey experiments, text analysis, and network methods to study elite political behavior and the role of information and expertise in policymaking.
